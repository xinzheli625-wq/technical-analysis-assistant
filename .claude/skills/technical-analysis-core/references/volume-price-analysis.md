# Volume-Price Analysis (VSA) Reference

## Core Principle

Volume reveals the intent behind price moves. Price is what happened; volume reveals who caused it.

## Key Signals

| Signal | Price | Volume | Meaning |
|--------|-------|--------|---------|
| Accumulation | Narrow range, slight up | Above average | Smart money buying |
| Distribution | Narrow range, slight down | Above average | Smart money selling |
| Markup | Strong up | High + increasing | Confirmed uptrend |
| Markdown | Strong down | High + increasing | Confirmed downtrend |
| Test | Pullback to support | Low | Supply exhausted |
| No Supply | Small up after decline | Very low | No sellers left |
| Upthrust | Quick spike above resistance | High, then close back | Trap, likely down |

## Volume Rules

1. **Breakouts need volume confirmation.** A breakout on low volume is suspect.
2. **Volume precedes price.** Volume often changes direction before price does.
3. **Declining volume on pullbacks in uptrend is healthy.** Shows no distribution.
4. **Volume spikes at tops/bottoms often mark reversals.** Especially if price reverses same day.
5. **Compare to 20-day average.** Volume ratio > 2x = significant; < 0.5x = quiet.
